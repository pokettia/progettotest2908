Porta San Donato (also known as Porta Zamboni)

Porta San Donato is one of the medieval gates of Bologna, located in the university district. It served both a defensive function and a role in regulating access to the city. The gate is also called Porta Zamboni after the street leading to it.

SPARQL Query 1: Retrieve the Gate

The following query searches for the monument labeled as Porta San Donato in Bologna. 

PREFIX rdf:  <http://www.w3.org/1999/02/22-rdf-syntax-ns#>
PREFIX rdfs: <http://www.w3.org/2000/01/rdf-schema#>
PREFIX arco: <https://w3id.org/arco/ontology/arco/>
PREFIX arco-core: <https://w3id.org/arco/ontology/core/>

SELECT DISTINCT ?porta ?label
WHERE {
  ?porta rdf:type arco:ArchitecturalOrLandscapeHeritage .
  ?porta rdfs:label ?label .
  FILTER (REGEX(STR(?label), "Porta San Donato", "i"))
  FILTER (REGEX(STR(?label), "Bologna", "i"))
}
LIMIT 100

SPARQL Query 2: DESCRIBE

To collect all RDF triples related to this monument, the following DESCRIBE query can be used with its unique identifier. 

PREFIX arco: <https://w3id.org/arco/ontology/arco/>

DESCRIBE ?s
WHERE {
  ?s arco:uniqueIdentifier "0800241601" .
}
ORDER BY ?s
LIMIT 200

Discussion

The SPARQL queries confirm the classification of Porta San Donato as architectural heritage within the ArCo dataset. The metadata retrieved provide insight into its identifiers, historical context, and relation to the city of Bologna. The alternative name Porta Zamboni demonstrates the gate's strong connection to the university district.

Conclusion

Porta San Donato represents both a historical and cultural landmark of Bologna. By leveraging Linked Open Data and SPARQL queries, detailed information can be systematically retrieved, enabling comparative analysis with other medieval gates.
